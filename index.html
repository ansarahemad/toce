"Economical Design of Earthquake-Resistant Buildings" project

The team of AAKAR, IIT Bombay declared our Civil Engineering students’ project - Economical Design of Earthquake-Resistant Buildings as 2nd rank at national level with awards. It had been structured on Base Isolation Technique and tested by adding deadweight of 500 gm. at the building’s first and top floor then fixed to a shake table with two accelerometers to the base of building (not base plate) and at the top and center of beams. In the form of ground motion, applied frequency of 8Hz to 15Hz dynamic motion and peak acceleration of 2G to the footing structure for 60 seconds duration. Accelerometers recorded the drift of the slide bearings that allow the foundation to move without moving the structure above it which results far less deformation and damage and no changes in superstructure is required unlike other techniques.
